# COLLECTIBLES.md — Dark Horizons: Legends of Lost Kingdoms

> The world is full of things that were left behind.
> Collecting them is not hoarding. It's preservation.

---

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Collectibles serve three purposes:

1. **Exploration reward** — Finding a hidden chest or obscure landmark feels good. Collectibles make that feeling tangible.
2. **Lore delivery** — The game's deepest story is told through collectibles, not cutscenes. Players who collect find out what actually happened.
3. **World density** — Collectibles make the world feel inhabited and purposeful. Every corner can have something.

**The rule:** A collectible should tell a story or serve a purpose. "A rock you found" is not a collectible. "A fragment of the Aethermount Scholar's Field Notes, written three days before the Dark Horizon event" is a collectible.

## COLLECTIBLE CATEGORIES

### CATEGORY 1 — HIDDEN CHESTS

**Total:** 100 chests across the world
**Tracker:** Petra Vine's building ("You have [X] of 100 hidden chests")

Hidden chests are the most distributed collectible type. They are physical objects in the world — small wooden or stone chests that the player must navigate to find. They are not marked on any map.

**Contents:** Each hidden chest contains:
- A small Zen Coin amount (10–100 based on difficulty/location)
- One piece of lore (text entry) from the region's history
- Sometimes: A crafting material (rare)
- Occasionally: A relic (see Category 2)

**Chest types by region:**
| Region | Count | Visual Style | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Castle / Castle Town | 10 | Ironbound wood | Easy (visible) |
| North Forest | 12 | Moss-covered wood | Medium (hidden in undergrowth) |
| Mining area | 8 | Stone-carved | Easy (in ore caves) |
| River / Waterfall | 8 | Waterlogged wood | Medium (requires finding ledges) |
| Mountain Zone | 10 | Iron-reinforced | Hard (require traversal) |
| Snow Biome | 10 | Frost-covered | Hard (require visual search in white terrain) |
| Desert | 8 | Sand-worn | Medium (partially buried) |
| Swamp | 8 | Rotten wood (still locked) | Hard (hidden in dark areas) |
| East Coast | 8 | Sea-chest style | Medium |
| South Beaches | 5 | Simple wood | Easy |
| Secret areas | 13 | Varies (themed to secret) | Very hard |

**Chest reset:** Daily at sunrise (GAME_RULES.md). After resetting, the same chest can be found again, but lore entries are not re-delivered — only Zen Coins and materials.

### CATEGORY 2 — RELICS

**Total:** 35 relics
**Tracker:** Elder Maerik's archive, Petra Vine's display cases

Relics are unique items — each one exists once in the world. They cannot be farmed. They are found in specific locations and do not reset.

**Relic sub-types:**

#### Kingdom Relics (10 total — 2 per kingdom)
Objects that belonged to one of the five kingdoms before the Dark Horizon event. Finding both relics from a kingdom unlocks a kingdom-specific lore sequence from Elder Maerik and illuminates the corresponding crest on the Oath Stone.

| Kingdom | Relic 1 | Relic 2 | Location hints |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stonehaven | The Stonehaven Mason's Level | The Iron Covenant Document | Mining area + Castle basement |
| Verdania | The Verdanian Tamer's Handbook | Dr. Solaine's Lost Page | North Forest + River Town |
| Solara | The Solaran Trade Ledger | Cass Orin's Old Key | South Coast + Port 1 |
| Aethermount | The Crystal Scholar's Compass | The Star Chart Fragment | Mountain + Snow |
| Celestis | The White Tower Bell Shard | The Last Celestian's Letter | Castle Library + Secret area |

#### Dungeon Relics (9 total — 3 per dungeon)
Each dungeon has 3 relics. One on Floor 1, one on Floor 3, one in the boss room. They are not on the floor — they're hidden in the dungeon environment (in alcoves, behind false walls, in containers).

Finding all 3 from a dungeon unlocks a unique title for the player and a Petra Vine display case dedicated to that dungeon.

#### Exploration Relics (16 total)
Found in the world's hidden and hard-to-reach locations. Not tied to any kingdom or dungeon — they're the world's residual history.

Examples:
- The Broken Compass (near the Lighthouse, 3rd floor)
- The Sailor's Logbook (washed up on South Beach, behind the tideline)
- The Ancient Seed (Botanical Archive ruins)
- The Fossilized Paw Print (large — from something that does not exist anymore)
- The Cracked Mirror Fragment (Mirror Lake — visible only during Sunrise)
- The Unnamed Grave Marker (swamp — barely visible)

### CATEGORY 3 — JOURNAL PAGES

**Total:** 50 journal pages
**Tracker:** Elder Maerik's archive (each page delivered = one entry)

Journal pages are torn fragments of documents written before, during, and after the Dark Horizon event. They tell the story from multiple perspectives — scholars, soldiers, traders, children, and one perspective that should not be possible (pages written after everyone was gone).

**Distribution:**
- Pages 1–15: Found in the overworld (accessible from Awesome rank)
- Pages 16–30: Found in biomes (Mountain, Snow, Desert, Swamp — require higher ranks)
- Pages 31–40: Found in dungeons
- Pages 41–50: Found in secret and endgame locations

**Reading experience:** When a page is delivered to Elder Maerik, he reads it aloud (text display). He adds commentary. Sometimes his commentary is wrong — he misinterprets what the page says, and the player can tell it's wrong from other pages they've found. This is intentional.

**The 50th page:** Written in a hand that matches no one in the archive. The paper is newer than it should be. Elder Maerik goes very quiet. He sets it on the table. He does not add it to the archive.

### CATEGORY 4 — KINGDOM CRESTS

**Total:** 5 crests (one per kingdom)
**Purpose:** Bring all 5 to the Oath Stone in the castle courtyard

Each crest is the carved stone seal of one of the five kingdoms. They were scattered across the world in the chaos of the Dark Horizon event. Finding and placing all five in the Oath Stone is the game's primary long-term goal for lore completion.

**Crest locations:**
- Stonehaven Crest: In the Mining Area — behind the Mechanical Crusher (requires God rank + Grend's quest)
- Verdanian Crest: In the Deep Forest — at the base of the Guardian Tree (requires Heavens rank + pet catch in the Guardian's area)
- Solaran Crest: At the Beast Port — requires completing Cass's full quest arc
- Aethermount Crest: Snow Biome — inside the Ice Garden maze (requires Over Heavens rank)
- Celestis Crest: Inside the sealed Throne Room — requires Dark Horizon rank

**Oath Stone effect:** Placing all 5 crests simultaneously (they must all be in the player's inventory) triggers a world event unique to that player. The stone glows. All five kingdom colors appear. Elder Maerik, Aria, and Tal Rensh all independently walk to the stone and stand at its edges. No dialogue. The player understands without being told: this has not happened in 300 years.

### CATEGORY 5 — ORE SAMPLES

**Total:** 10 samples (2 per ore type)
**Purpose:** Dr. Solaine's compendium

A unique version of each ore type — found not in the mine but at specific environmental locations where the ore is exposed naturally. Finding both samples of an ore type gives Dr. Solaine data for her monograph and yields a small Zen Coin bonus.

| Ore | Sample 1 Location | Sample 2 Location |
|---|---|---|
| Stone | Cliffside vein (visible, easy) | Riverbed (Rain reveals it) |
| Iron | Mountain rockslide debris | Mechanical Crusher access point |
| Crystal | Waterfall mist zone (behind spray) | Crystal Cavern inner chamber |
| Mystic | Snow biome ice formation | Dungeon 2 Floor 2 |
| Dark | Dungeon 3 Floor 4 | The Horizon Stone's base |

### CATEGORY 6 — BOTANICAL SPECIMENS

**Total:** 20 plants
**Purpose:** Dr. Solaine's pressed flower collection (display case in her workspace)

Each specimen is an interactable plant in the world. The player picks (interacts), it's added to inventory, and Dr. Solaine accepts it for the compendium. Some plants only appear in specific weather or time conditions.

Notable specimens:
- Moonbloom (forest, midnight only)
- Desert Rose Formation (desert, full sun)
- Frost Lily (snow biome, Snow weather)
- Ghost Flower (swamp, Fog weather only)
- Waterfall Moss (river zone, in the spray zone — requires rain to access the ledge)
- Midnight Mushroom (cave, accessible only after mining that node cluster)
- The Unnamed Black Flower (grows only at the Horizon Stone's base — not in the database anywhere)

### CATEGORY 7 — STARCHART FRAGMENTS

**Total:** 12 fragments
**Purpose:** Reassembled into a complete star map at the Star Circle (Landmark #14)

Each fragment is a piece of an ancient astronomical map used by Aethermount scholars to track the stars. When all 12 fragments are combined at the Stone Circle during Aurora weather, the assembled chart reveals something about the constellation that will appear at the game's climax.

Fragments are found:
- 3 in the Snow Biome
- 3 in the Mountain Zone
- 2 in Dungeon 3
- 2 in the Castle Library (Elder Maerik has them but won't give them to the player until Heavens rank — he's protective of the archive)
- 2 in the Secret Observatory (requires finding a hidden entrance on the mountain)

---

### CATEGORY 8 — BLOOD MOON RECORDS

**Total:** Unlimited (accumulates)
**Purpose:** Loryn's notice board archive, Petra Vine's Blood Moon log

After each Blood Moon, a record is added:
- Which Blood Moon number this was (for this player)
- What Legendary encounters occurred
- Player's activities during the event

**Milestone rewards:**
- 1st Blood Moon: Unique title ("Moonwatcher")
- 10th Blood Moon: A Blood Moon Record document from 300 years ago (showing the very first Blood Moon in the archive)
- 50th Blood Moon: The final Blood Moon record. Written in the same handwriting as the 50th journal page. Impossible.

---

### CATEGORY 9 — POSTCARDS FROM JED FARREN

**Total:** 30 postcards
**Purpose:** Humor, lore texture, Petra's display

Jed Farren has been everywhere and writes enthusiastic, slightly inaccurate postcards. Each postcard is found at a landmark after the player has visited it. The postcard describes the landmark from Jed's perspective — which is always slightly different from what the player actually saw.

Example:
> "The Waterfall — INCREDIBLE. The spray soaks you completely. I have determined the fish are friendly. The fish were NOT friendly. Send boots. —Jed"

Example:
> "The Sealed Throne Room — managed to get close to the bars. Saw the silhouette. Have decided not to think about it. Highly recommend. —Jed"

## TROPHY ROOM DISPLAY SYSTEM

Petra Vine manages the physical display of all collectibles in the castle's Trophy Room. As the player finds items, the Trophy Room fills:

**Display sections:**
- Wall cases: Kingdom relics (10 slots)
- Central table: The assembled Starchart (when complete)
- Shelf rows: Journal pages (50 slots, individually readable)
- Specimen drawers: Ore samples and botanical specimens
- Pin board: Jed Farren's postcards
- Chest display: Hidden chests (represented by region maps with markers)
- Crest alcove: Five stone slots for the Kingdom Crests

**Empty state design:** The Trophy Room at game start is not sad — it's expectant. The display cases are clean, the labels are printed, the lights are on. The room is ready. It was always going to be filled.

# DAY_NIGHT_SYSTEM.md — Dark Horizons: Legends of Lost Kingdoms

> The world changes with the light.
> Day and night are not cosmetic. They change who is home, what is awake, and what is possible.

---

## CYCLE OVERVIEW

One full day/night cycle = **40 minutes real time** (configurable).

| Phase | Duration | Real Time |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | Sunrise → full day | 6 min |
| Day | Full brightness | 12 min |
| Evening | Day → sunset colors | 6 min |
| Night | Dark world | 10 min |
| Midnight | Darkest point | 6 min |

Total: 40 minutes. Players who log in at any time enter the cycle at the current server time position. The cycle is server-authoritative — all players share the same time of day.

---

## PHASE 1 — MORNING

**Time:** Cycle minutes 0–6

**Lighting:**
- Sky transitions from deep blue-black to warm rose-gold
- Horizon line (east) brightens first — the Dark Horizon is briefly, unusually visible in silhouette against the rose light
- Terrain colors shift from cool blue-grey to warm gold
- Ambient particles become gold-tinted
- Zone markers pulse once at sunrise (a morning acknowledgment)

**Music:**
- Morning Theme: Starts with a solo instrument (flute or harp) playing a simple ascending melody over silence. Strings join gradually. By the end of the 6 minutes, the full Morning orchestration has built.
- Volume: Starts low, rises gently with the sun.

**NPC Schedules:**
- Aria Vayne: Arrives at the castle gate, coffee substitute in hand, looking east
- Cass Orin: Opens his stall (morning check of inventory, reorganizes visually)
- Grend Voss: Lights the forge — visible smoke from the blacksmith chimney
- Elder Maerik: Found reading in the library window, not yet receiving visitors
- Jed Farren: Leaves his most recent location heading toward the day's new position

**Pet Behavior:**
- Nocturnal pets (bats, shadow-type, undead spirits) retreat to dens
- Diurnal pets become active: woodland creatures, hawks, butterflies
- Morning has a 2-minute "golden window" where encounter rate is +15% (pets are active but haven't scattered yet)
- The Crow Tower crows wake and begin circling (ambient animation)

**Special Events:**
- Sunrise daily reset: Hidden chests that have been collected reset at sunrise
- Daily quests refresh at sunrise
- The Salmon Run (Landmark #33): Morning only — salmon jumping the waterfall visible (future interaction)

## PHASE 2 — DAY

**Time:** Cycle minutes 6–18

**Lighting:**
- Full world brightness — terrain colors at maximum saturation
- Sky: Deep blue with small cloud particles (passive, not rain weather)
- Shadows under trees at correct angle
- The Sundial (Landmark #8) reads correctly throughout this phase
- Long shadows in the desert (harsh midday look)

**Music:**
- Day Theme: Full orchestral overworld piece. Uplifting but not aggressive. Sense of possibility and openness. Approximately 6-minute loop with variations — forest path variation (low strings, woodwinds), mountain path variation (brass, percussion), coastal variation (oceanic instruments).
- The music adapts slightly based on which terrain zone the player is in.

**NPC Schedules:**
- All NPCs at their primary locations
- Aria: Castle gate or in the library (alternates)
- Grend: At the forge — visible and active
- Dr. Solaine: At her table in River Town
- Elder Maerik: Receiving visitors in the library
- Jed: At his current day position (varies)
- Loryn: At the notice board
- Quest Master, Historian, Blacksmith, Merchant: Full availability

**Pet Behavior:**
- Standard encounter rates apply
- Diurnal pets: Maximum activity (hawks, foxes, woodland sprites, flower pets)
- Desert pets most active during day
- Mountain pets active in early-day, retreating to shade from midday onward
- The Butterfly Garden most active during day

**Special Events:**
- Sundial puzzle: At cycle minute 12 (noon equivalent), the Sundial shadow aligns with the "Roll at twelfth mark" inscription — visiting the Roll Shrine at this exact time gives a small bonus
- Desert Sphinx riddle: Only accessible during Day phase

## PHASE 3 — EVENING

**Time:** Cycle minutes 18–24

**Lighting:**
- Sky shifts from blue to deep orange-gold on the west horizon
- East sky remains blue-grey — the contrast between east and west is dramatic and deliberate (east horizon never fully brightens — the Dark Horizon prevents it)
- Terrain: Warm orange-gold wash over everything
- Zone markers: Glow noticeably brighter as ambient light drops
- The Lantern Path lights its lanterns (they kindle one by one, south to north)
- Crystal Fountain cycles to its gold phase (aligns with evening light)

**Music:**
- Evening Theme: Slower, warmer. The full orchestration from day is reduced to strings and piano. A sense of things winding down with beauty. Not sad — reflective.
- Birdsong transitions from day birds to evening birds (different call pattern)
- Distant wolf-like call becomes audible from the northwest at the very end of evening

**NPC Schedules:**
- Aria: Moves to the castle garden, sits on the bench near the Knight statue
- Cass: Closing his stall, but happy to talk over the box-packing (full dialogue available)
- Grend: Banking the forge, stepping outside for air
- Jed: Heading toward whatever evening landmark he's observing
- Dr. Solaine: Still at her table, more distracted (harder to get full attention)
- Loryn: Reviewing the day's quests (will give tomorrow's quest preview)

**Pet Behavior:**
- Crepuscular pets: Peak activity (foxes, owls, deer-type pets)
- Dr. Solaine mentioned this in her dialogue — paying attention to NPCs pays off
- Evening is the second-best time for encounters (behind morning golden window and night-specific spawns)
- Desert pets transition — day species retreat, night species begin emerging
- The Beehive Oak glows amber as bees return for the night

**Special Events:**
- Evening: The Stone Circle (north forest) begins its glow preparation — the stones warm up visually
- The fishing pools near the river become most active (future fishing mechanic)
- Evening roll bonus: One hour before Blood Moon (if Blood Moon is tonight), the Roll Shrine begins glowing red — warning the player

## PHASE 4 — NIGHT

**Time:** Cycle minutes 24–34

**Lighting:**
- Full darkness — sky becomes deep blue-black with star particles
- World ambient light: Low — enough to navigate, creates atmosphere
- Zone markers: Full brightness (the primary navigation aids at night)
- Lantern Path, Castle Lanterns: Fully lit, casting warm gold halos
- The Fairy Ring glows at amber intensity
- The Ghost Lights in the swamp are highly visible
- The Crystal Fountain slows and shifts to a deep blue glow
- The River shimmers with reflected starlight
- The five Eternal Lamps become the dominant light sources in their respective areas

**Music:**
- Night Theme: Ambient, mysterious. Piano with very sparse instrumentation. Long silences between musical moments. The silence is intentional — night has space in it.
- Occasional wind sweep sound (not storm — just emptiness)
- Owl calls
- The waterfall sounds louder at night (same volume, but everything else is quieter)

**NPC Schedules:**
- Most NPCs "off duty" — visual indicator shows them inside their buildings (lights in windows)
- Available at night: Aria (walks the castle wall), Jed (night observation), Ravel (Dungeon 1 entrance — he guards at night), Tal Rensh (always at the dock)
- Closed: Loryn (notice board unstaffed), Grend (forge dark), Cass (stall closed)
- Night: Dr. Solaine leaves a note on her table if the player arrives: "Night rounds. Try the east river. —M.S."

**Pet Behavior:**
- Nocturnal pets emerge: Bats, shadow creatures, undead spirits, night flowers
- Diurnal pets retreat and are very hard to find at night
- The Swamp's full encounter table activates at night (highest encounter rate in the swamp)
- Mountain zone: Stone Giants become faintly luminescent — hint that they're not quite inert
- The Fairy Ring: Night encounter window opens (15-minute window in the middle of Night phase)

**Special Events:**
- Night Fishing (future mechanic): River most productive at night
- Sea encounters at ports: Visible lights on the water suggesting sea creatures near the shore
- The Roll Altar burns brighter at night — night rolls have a visual upgrade but no mechanical difference
- Hidden area glow: All secret zone hidden areas have a very faint shimmer at night (navigational fairness)
- One night per cycle, a distant light moves across the horizon from east to west — unexplained. It has been moving across the horizon every night for three hundred years.

## PHASE 5 — MIDNIGHT

**Time:** Cycle minutes 34–40

**Lighting:**
- Sky at maximum darkness
- Stars at maximum density
- If Aurora weather: Midnight Aurora is the most dramatic variant
- If Blood Moon: Midnight Blood Moon is peak intensity
- World detail becomes silhouette-heavy — shapes rather than colors
- The Dark Horizon on the eastern horizon is most clearly visible at midnight — the contrast between the dark world and the darker horizon is the subtlest point of the cycle
- The Horizon Stone glows very faintly at midnight (it has absorbed something from the east)

**Music:**
- Midnight Theme: A single held drone note. Not melodic. Then, at exactly midnight, a brief complete silence of 3 seconds. Then the drone resumes. The silence is designed to make players notice something changed.
- In the silence: the waterfall stops audibly for exactly 3 seconds. Then resumes.
- (The waterfall does not actually stop. This is a sound design trick. Players who notice get no reward. The noticing is the reward.)

**NPC Schedules:**
- All NPCs asleep or unavailable except: Ravel Kost (paces slowly near Dungeon 1) and Tal Rensh (never leaves the dock)
- The Bell Tower: If a player finds a way to ring the bell at midnight (future mechanic), it has unique effects
- Aria is seen briefly at the castle wall looking east — if approached, she says one line and goes inside. The line changes with player rank.

**Pet Behavior:**
- Rarest nocturnal pets appear only at midnight
- Legendary encounters: Some are midnight-only (specifically: Shadow-type Legendaries, the Willow Wraith)
- The Fairy Ring is inert at midnight (its window is during Night phase)
- Underground: Cave creatures maximum activity (if cave is entered at midnight, all nodes immediately active)
- Snow Biome: Glacier Colossus most active at midnight

**Special Events:**
- Midnight Reset: The cycle ends and Morning begins. The transition from midnight darkness to sunrise is the most dramatic visual in the game — the sky lightens from absolute dark to rose gold in 30 seconds.
- The Oath Stone pulses once at midnight — visible from anywhere in the castle grounds.
- Daily wishing pool reset: At midnight, the accumulated coins in the pool reset (fresh pool, fresh interaction odds).
- The five shrine Legendary trigger resets at midnight — fresh chance for each new Night/Midnight cycle.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

**Cycle timer:**
The day/night cycle is driven by a server-side timestamp. The client calculates current phase from `(Date.now() - cycleStartTimestamp) % CYCLE_DURATION_MS`.

**CSS class approach:**
Similar to weather — a `time-*` class on the world canvas container adjusts ambient light overlays, object brightness, and particle systems.

**Music transitions:**
Phase transitions fade current music track out over 30 seconds while the new track fades in. Tracks loop during their phase. Looping point is designed to be seamless (4-bar intro before the loop point).

**NPC visibility:**
NPCs have a `schedule` array defining which phases they are visible. The NPC renderer checks `currentPhase` against the NPC's schedule before rendering. If not in schedule, the NPC building shows a "light on inside" indicator instead.

**React context structure:**
```typescript
interface TimeState {
  phase: "morning" | "day" | "evening" | "night" | "midnight";
  cycleProgress: number; // 0–1 through full cycle
  phaseProgress: number; // 0–1 through current phase
  minuteOfDay: number;   // 0–39 (40-min cycle)
}
```

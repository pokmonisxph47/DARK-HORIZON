# NPC_BIBLE.md — Dark Horizons: Legends of Lost Kingdoms

> Every NPC in this document is a character, not a menu.
> They have a past, a personality, an agenda, and a reason to be standing where they are.
> Dialogue should always feel like talking to a specific person — not reading a help tooltip.

---

## NPC DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**The Three-Line Rule:** Every NPC can be summarized in three lines:
1. Who they are (personality, background)
2. What they want (their goal, not their function)
3. What they know that the player needs (their gameplay role, stated as knowledge)

NPCs who feel like people make a world feel inhabited. NPCs who feel like menus make a world feel like a spreadsheet.

**Dialogue tone:** Cinzel Decorative fantasy, but grounded. No "thy" or "thee." No "forsooth." These are people who live in a broken world and are doing their best. Dignity, humor, weariness, occasional wonder.

## NPC 1 — THE GUIDE (ARIA VAYNE)
**Location:** Castle Gate, appears when player first enters Home Castle zone
**Role:** Tutorial companion, world explainer
**Appearance:** A young woman in practical travel clothes — worn leather jacket, ink-stained fingers, a satchel overflowing with maps and notes. Hair pulled back. One earring — a small carved stone in the shape of a pine tree (Verdanian style).

**Personality:** Intensely curious and slightly disorganized. She knows more than she lets on and delivers information in layers — enough to get started, more when it matters. She finds the player interesting rather than needing them. She is excited to see what they will discover.

**Background:** Aria is the granddaughter of one of the last Verdanian tamers. She grew up in the castle's library, reading everything she could find about the old kingdoms. She became the castle's unofficial guide because no one else wanted the job and she was the only one who knew the answers.

**Dialogue style:** Fast, punctuated by self-interruption. "Right, so — the castle was built by Stonehaven, which is — actually, you don't need to know all of that. The short version: it's yours now. Mostly. Come on."

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Castle orientation, controls introduction (M4)
- Second arc: The Archive Hunt — Aria needs specific journal pages from across the world to complete the history of the Dark Horizon event (M7+)
- Final arc: Aria reveals she has been to the Horizon Stone. She did not like what she saw. (M9)

**Trivia:** Aria keeps a personal journal that updates with player progress. Finding it (hidden in the castle library) reveals her private thoughts about the player.

## NPC 2 — THE MERCHANT (CASS ORIN)
**Location:** Castle Town, Merchant Quarter — behind a stall filled with goods under cloth covers
**Role:** Item shop (Zen Coins for useful items), future trading hub
**Appearance:** A heavyset older man with an impressive grey beard and an extraordinary hat — wide-brimmed, slightly crushed on one side, adorned with pins from every port in the world. He wears rings on every finger and smells like sea salt.

**Personality:** Cheerful, mercenary, and deeply practical. He loves a good deal more than he loves money — the negotiation is the thing. He respects players who ask direct questions and gives better hints to players who have explored far.

**Background:** Cass was a Solaran trading captain before the seas became dangerous. He sold his ship, kept the hat, and opened a stall. He knows more about the current state of the seas than anyone — he sent ships out to map them and only some came back.

**Dialogue style:** Warm, faintly salesman-like, with occasional unexpected depth. "Forty Zen Coins for the compass. No, I won't come down. That compass belonged to my first mate and she'd want a fair price. Forty."

**Inventory (when shop opens, M7):**
- Navigation compass (reduces sea navigation time)
- Fog lamp (improves visibility in fog weather)
- Bait bundles (increases river encounter chance)
- Blank journal pages (for collectible system)
- "Mystery crate" (random item, 200 Zen Coins)

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Cass sends the player to find proof that the 3rd Sea is navigable (M8)
- Second arc: His last ship hasn't returned — he asks the player to find it in the Beast Sea (M8)
- Final arc: Cass is on the last ship. He survived. He's not ready to talk about what he found. (M9)

## NPC 3 — THE BLACKSMITH (GREND VOSS)
**Location:** Castle Town, workshop to the right of the Castle Bell Tower
**Role:** Armor system, equipment upgrades, restoration quests
**Appearance:** Enormous. Not tall — just very wide. Short red hair, one eyebrow scar, hands like geological formations. Always in a leather apron over a Stonehaven-crest shirt. He is somehow always too warm.

**Personality:** Blunt, low-key funny, absolutely reliable. He does not over-promise. Everything he makes is better than he says it will be. He measures a person by whether they know what questions to ask.

**Background:** Grend's family has maintained the castle forge for seven generations. He knows the castle better than anyone except possibly Aria. He once repaired a crack in the castle wall that no one else could see and that would have compromised the wall's Dark Ore integrity. He does not talk about it.

**Dialogue style:** Short sentences. Direct. Occasionally fond. "That armor's seen better days. Leave it. Come back in a day." (It is ready in four hours. He wanted buffer time.)

**Services (M7+):**
- Armor upgrades (ore cost)
- Equipment repair
- Kingdom crest restoration (quest)
- The Mechanical Crusher activation quest

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Grend asks the player to bring him a specific ore configuration to test the forge's compatibility with Dark Ore (M7)
- Second arc: The Knight's Garden restoration — Grend repairs the broken statue arm (M8)
- Final arc: Grend forges the Horizon Ward — a one-time item that provides protection inside Dungeon 3. It costs everything. (M9)

## NPC 4 — THE PET PROFESSOR (DR. MIRA SOLAINE)
**Location:** Fisher's Rest (River Town), at a table covered in specimen jars and pressed flowers
**Role:** Pet encyclopedia, catch rate advice, pet evolution hints
**Appearance:** A slight older woman in deep green robes, spectacles with three lenses (she flips between them for different magnifications), and a pet on each shoulder — a small owl on the left, a tiny fox on the right. Neither is hostile. Both are watching the player.

**Personality:** Academic, distracted, brilliant. She finishes other people's sentences (incorrectly). She becomes fully focused when talking about a species she's never seen before. She argues with her own conclusions.

**Background:** Dr. Solaine is the last living student of the Verdanian taming tradition. She has documented 47 pet species. She suspects there are at least 100 more. Her primary goal is to see every species before she dies — she estimates this will take three more decades and is "cautiously optimistic."

**Dialogue style:** Dense with information, interrupted by digressions. "The Forest Fox is crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk — which is why you're not finding them at midday. They also prefer rain, which you would know if you'd read my monograph, which I assume you haven't, but—" (The player has not read it. It is findable as a collectible.)

**Services:**
- Pet identification (what a caught pet's abilities will be at higher tiers)
- Biome-specific encounter advice
- Pet behavior tips (time-of-day and weather optimization)
- Pet "appraisal" — she identifies which pets are unusually strong specimens

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Bring her three specimens she's never seen before (Rare or higher tier, different biomes) (M5)
- Second arc: The Ice Garden photo — she wants a sketch of every sculpture in the snow biome's Ice Garden (M9)
- Final arc: Dr. Solaine's final monograph, completed with the player's data, becomes a collectible item. She cries a little. (Post-M9)

## NPC 5 — THE DUNGEON MASTER (RAVEL KOST)
**Location:** Near Dungeon 1 entrance (1810, 460), standing at the edge of the path to the arch
**Role:** Dungeon preparation, lore about dungeon mechanics, difficulty calibration hints
**Appearance:** Tall and very still. Dark robes with no crest — he serves no kingdom. He has a very long scar running from his left temple to his chin that he has never explained. He carries a staff topped with a sealed dark crystal.

**Personality:** Serious, measured, occasionally frightening. He never warns players away from dungeons — he prepares them instead. He believes the dungeons can and should be cleared and is frustrated that most people refuse to try. He respects courage and preparation equally.

**Background:** Ravel tried to clear Dungeon 3 once. He got to Floor 5. He came back changed and has never spoken about what he saw there. He has been preparing others to succeed where he didn't ever since.

**Dialogue style:** Deliberate. He pauses before speaking. "You're not ready." (Pause.) "That is not an insult. It is an observation. Here is what ready looks like."

**Services:**
- Pre-dungeon briefing (unique tips for each dungeon's mechanic)
- Floor maps (purchasable with Zen Coins — partial maps that help navigate)
- Post-dungeon debrief ("What did you learn?")
- The corrupted crystal staff — he will explain it if asked 3+ times

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Ravel asks the player to bring back a specific item from Dungeon 1's Floor 3 — something he dropped when he ran (M9)
- Second arc: He accompanies the player to Dungeon 2's entrance but won't go inside. He tells them what's in there. He is very precise about what's in there. (M9)
- Final arc: When the player enters Dungeon 3, Ravel's voice is the last they hear before the gate closes. "Whatever you find in there — remember it was always in there. The darkness didn't put it there. We did." (M9)

## NPC 6 — THE QUEST MASTER (LORYN ASHVALE)
**Location:** Castle Town, central building with a notice board outside
**Role:** Quest assignment, daily/weekly challenge tracking, reward distribution
**Appearance:** A woman in her mid-thirties with a no-nonsense posture and a clipboard she always has but never looks at. She wears the combined crest of all five kingdoms — a sign that she serves the castle as a neutral institution, not any one tradition.

**Personality:** Efficient, dry-witted, secretly delighted by extraordinary players. She has seen everything and is impressed by almost nothing — which makes it meaningful when she is.

**Background:** Loryn was appointed Quest Master by the castle council (three NPCs who don't appear until multiplayer) as a compromise candidate after the previous Quest Master attempted to send players to the Beast Sea at rank Noob "as a learning experience."

**Dialogue style:** Concise. Occasionally editorializes. "Dungeon cleared, both chests, floor time under twelve minutes. Third fastest this month. The other two were pets, which I'm still arguing about."

**Services:**
- Daily quests (resets at real-time midnight)
- Weekly challenges (more complex, higher rewards)
- Milestone bounties ("First Legendary caught this week")
- World event announcement board

**Quest arcs:**
- Running arc: Loryn slowly reveals the notice board's history — each older layer of papers tells a story from the castle's past. Removing old papers (interaction) reveals progressively older notices beneath.

## NPC 7 — THE SEA CAPTAIN (TAL RENSH)
**Location:** Port 1 (Sunrise Dock), sitting on a barrel looking at the sea
**Role:** Sea voyage briefing, sea-layer preparation, Beast Port hint
**Appearance:** Weatherbeaten, confident, looks about 50 but has been 50 for as long as anyone in the castle can remember. One prosthetic arm (left) that she built herself from Stonehaven iron. The arm has a compass built into the wrist.

**Personality:** Calm. Infinitely calm. She has survived four seas and the collapse of Solara's entire trading empire. She is not easily alarmed. She is also the only person in the castle who looks at the eastern horizon without any visible emotion — just assessment.

**Background:** Tal was one of the last captains of the Solaran trading fleet. She was the first person to sail all four seas and return. She does not discuss the Beast Sea unless directly asked, and even then only once.

**Dialogue style:** Maritime metaphors, understated. "The first sea is calm. Don't let it teach you the wrong things."

**Services:**
- Sea voyage briefing (what to expect in each sea)
- Navigation advice (weather effects on sea encounters)
- Beast Port location hint (given only to Over Heavens rank players who ask three times)
- The missing ship quest (Cass's questline cross-reference)

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Tal asks the player to prove they can survive the first sea before she tells them anything about the second (M8)
- Final arc: Tal is at the Horizon Stone when the player arrives for the final act. She has been there before. She has been waiting. (M9)

## NPC 8 — THE HISTORIAN (ELDER MAERIK)
**Location:** Castle library — a large chair surrounded by stacked books, visible from the castle garden
**Role:** Lore delivery, world history, collectible translation/identification
**Appearance:** Very old. White hair in a long braid. Reading glasses with seven different lenses on a rotating frame (even more elaborate than Dr. Solaine's). Hands that shake slightly when not holding a book. Never shakes when holding a book.

**Personality:** Patient, thorough, occasionally frustrated by people who want summaries. He believes the difference between knowing a thing and understanding it is exactly the time players don't want to spend. He meets players where they are, but always opens one door they didn't know was there.

**Background:** Elder Maerik was born in the castle and has never left. He knows more about the five kingdoms than anyone alive. He also knows things he hasn't told anyone — things he found in a sealed section of the castle archive that he has not opened in forty years.

**Dialogue style:** Measured, warm, occasionally wandering. "The Aethermount scholars — yes, that journal page you found is theirs. I can tell by the ink color. They used a pigment made from Crystal ore, which is why it glows slightly in the dark. Did you notice that? No? Go look. I'll wait."

**Services:**
- Journal page translation and archive entry
- Kingdom crest identification
- Rune translation (snow biome obelisk, dungeon inscriptions)
- The sealed archive section (will not open until M9)

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Bring back any three journal pages from outside the castle (M5)
- Running arc: Each journal page delivered reveals one chapter of the sealed archive history
- Final arc: Elder Maerik opens the sealed section. What's inside explains everything about the Dark Horizon. He does not summarize. He hands the player the book. (M9)

## NPC 9 — THE ARENA MASTER (VIRA COLDSTONE)
**Location:** Titan's Arena exterior — pacing around the sealed entrance
**Role:** Future PvP/Raid gatekeeper, competitive combat framework
**Appearance:** Compact, scarred, moves like she's always ready for someone to attack her. Two short blades at her hips (decorative — no combat system yet). A tattoo on her neck of the Titan's Arena crest.

**Personality:** Competitive, professional, zero patience for excuses. She likes the player immediately if they've cleared a dungeon. She's dismissive until then.

**Background:** Vira was a dungeon clearer who retired because dungeon content wasn't challenging enough. She found the Titan's Arena and has been working on opening it ever since. She needs either a Raid team or a very specific key — one piece of which is in Dungeon 3.

**Dialogue style:** Direct, occasionally condescending. Warms up fast. "You cleared Dungeon 2? Interesting. Come back when you've done it in under ten minutes."

**Services (future):**
- PvP Arena matchmaking
- Raid lobby management
- Personal best records
- Arena cosmetic rewards

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Vira gives the player a challenge — clear any dungeon with a pet below the recommended power level. No explanation why. (M9)
- Final arc: Vira is the only NPC who can be recruited as a temporary combat companion for the Titan's Arena raid (M10+).

## NPC 10 — THE EXPLORER (JED FARREN)
**Location:** Roaming — appears in different locations each day
**Role:** Hints about secret areas, landmark locations, hidden chest positions
**Appearance:** Permanently disheveled. Excellent boots (the rest of the outfit is eclectic). An enormous pack of equipment. A hand-drawn map that he has very strong feelings about not sharing.

**Personality:** Enthusiastic, superstitious, occasionally wrong in endearing ways. He has opinions about every location in the world and will share them at length. Some of his information is accurate. Some is enthusiastic speculation. Players learn to cross-reference.

**Background:** Jed has been exploring the world for fifteen years. He has not cleared a dungeon (he tried once and decided it wasn't his kind of thing). He has found, by his own count, 43 secret locations. The actual number is 31.

**Dialogue style:** Excited, lists things, tangentially mentions relevant secrets. "The east coast — you've been there, right? The Lighthouse? Yes. Did you go inside? Most people don't go inside. There's a third floor. There's also a thing on the third floor. I'm not going to tell you what it is. It's better if you find it."

**Services:**
- Location-specific hints (one hint per real-world day per player)
- Secret chest refresh reminder (he tracks which hidden chests are ready to reset)
- Rare encounter time hints (he's observed spawn patterns for rare pets)

**Quest arcs:**
- Initial: Find Jed — he's in 10 different locations on a rotating schedule. Talking to him in all 10 locations completes an achievement.
- Running arc: Jed discovers secret areas the player hasn't found yet and posts notes at the nearest landmark.

## NPC 11 — THE COLLECTOR (PETRA VINE)
**Location:** Castle Town, in a small building packed floor-to-ceiling with display cases
**Role:** Collectible submission, completion tracking, reward distribution
**Appearance:** A young woman who is architecturally invisible behind the display cases — the player usually encounters her arm reaching out from behind a case before seeing her face. Meticulous, ink-stained hands, a ledger she updates constantly.

**Personality:** Obsessive, joyful, mildly terrifying about cataloguing. She has categories for her categories. She is the happiest NPC in the game when players bring things to her.

**Background:** Petra's family collected art. When the Dark Horizon happened, they lost everything. She now collects everything — not because she thinks she can replace what was lost but because she believes the act of collecting is itself a kind of preservation.

**Dialogue style:** Precise, enthusiastic, quickly clinical. "Desert Rose Formation sample? Yes. Third one this month — yours is the largest. I'm going to need to reclassify the display case. That's not a complaint."

**Services:**
- Collectible submission (journals, relics, ore samples, crests, etc.)
- Completion progress tracking ("You have 34 of 100 hidden chests")
- Achievement unlock notification
- Reward distribution for completed sets

**Quest arcs:**
- The Collected World: 50-stage questline that tracks every collectible category. Final stage: The player fills the last case in Petra's collection. She closes the ledger. She sits down. "That's all of them," she says. Then: "What do we collect now?" (Post-M9)
